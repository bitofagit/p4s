# Hydrology & Swales

Water is the lifeblood of the Pennines, but it is often too much or too little
at the wrong time.

## The Swale System

Swales are level‑bottomed trenches designed to stop water in its tracks. Instead
of water running off the clay and into the stream, a swale forces it to:

**Stop, Spread, and Soak.**

### Effects

- **Storage:** Swales hold moisture for the surrounding area.
- **Diffusion:** Moisture migrates into adjacent tiles over several turns.
- **Evaporation:** Uncovered swales lose water quickly.

### Strategy

Pair swales with canopy/groundcover structure to protect the water you caught.
See [[12_Understanding_Guilds]] for the vertical stacking principle.
