# The Biological Layer (The Web)
**Tags:** #mechanics #biology #soil-web

This is the life inside the soil. It is unpacked into three subcategories that reward distinct playstyles.

## 1. Fungal Load (The Mycorrhizal Network)
* **Role:** The internet of the soil. Connects tiles, shares water/minerals, and sends distress signals.
* **Loop:** A thirsty plant on a dry tile can physically pull Moisture from an adjacent wet tile via the network.
* **Vulnerability:** Extremely fragile. Instantly sliced to ribbons (reset to 0) if the player uses a rototiller, hoe, or pig tractor. Rewards "No-Till" perennial beds.

## 2. Microbial Activity (The Bacteria)
* **Role:** The stomachs of the soil. The nutrient unlockers.
* **Loop:** Plants cannot eat raw compost. High Bacterial activity acts as a multiplier, rapidly unlocking Fertility for heavy-feeding crops.
* **Vulnerability:** Short lifespans. Demands constant feeding (compost teas) or living roots in the ground.

## 3. Macro-Life (Earthworms & Arthropods)
* **Role:** The heavy lifters and automated engineers.
* **Loop:** High Macro-Life automatically increases the Structure and Topsoil Depth stats over time. The worms aerate the earth for you.
* **Vulnerability:** Drowns in waterlogged soil (Moisture 10) and bakes in bone-dry soil (Moisture 0).

## The "Vitality" Meta-Score
A single percentage score calculated by combining the three biological factors to represent the overall health of the Soil Food Web. If $F$, $B$, and $M$ are measured from 0 to 10:

$Vitality = \left( \frac{F \times B \times M}{1000} \right) \times 100\%$
