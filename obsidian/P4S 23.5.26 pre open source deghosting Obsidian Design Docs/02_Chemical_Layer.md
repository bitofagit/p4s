# The Chemical Layer (The Nutrients)
**Tags:** #mechanics #chemical #soil

These dictate the raw fuel available to the plants, whether they can access it, and if the ground is poisoned.

## Fertility (0-10)
The raw mineral and nutrient content of the soil.
* **Components:** N (Nitrogen), P (Phosphorus), K (Potassium), and Trace Minerals.
* **Dynamics:** Depleted by heavy-feeding annuals (corn, pumpkins). Restored by compost or nitrogen-fixing plants (clover, beans).

## pH Level (0-14)
The Gatekeeper stat. Kept in real-world measurements.
* **Mechanic:** Fertility means nothing if the pH is wrong for the specific crop.
* **Examples:** Blueberries demand highly acidic soil (3-4); Asparagus demands alkaline soil (8).

## Toxicity (0-10)
Heavy metals, chemical runoff, or industrial poisoning from the neighbouring farm.
* **Mechanic:** High Toxicity actively damages plants and completely nukes the [[03_Biological_Layer]].
* **Remediation:** Requires growing sacrificial phytoremediators (like Sunflowers) to pull the toxins out, which must then be safely disposed of.
