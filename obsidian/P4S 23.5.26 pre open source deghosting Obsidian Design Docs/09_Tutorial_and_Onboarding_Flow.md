# Permaculture 4 Squares: Tutorial & Onboarding Flow

The tutorial spans the first two in-game weeks. Instead of text dumps, the game teaches through ecological pressure: the land is hostile, the player fails slightly, and the game introduces a permaculture concept to solve it.

## Lesson 1: Observation & The Brittle Earth (Days 1-2)
* **The Concept:** You cannot force life into dead soil. Water is life.
* **The Pressure:** The player starts with a barren, compacted clay farm. They plant a seed, hit SLEEP, and immediately see the red `"Thirsty!"` warning as the seed dies from dry soil.
* **The Action:**
  1. Use the **Tile Inspector (Q)** to observe the `0.0 Moisture` and `Clay` tags.
  2. Equip the **Cultivate** tool to break the dirt.
  3. Plant a hardy Pioneer seed (like Daikon Radish).
  4. Use the **Watering Can (W)** to manually hydrate the tile before hitting SLEEP.

## Lesson 2: Earthworks & Passive Systems (Days 3-5)
* **The Concept:** Manual watering costs too much Energy. We must catch water in the landscape.
* **The Pressure:** The player runs out of Energy (`0/20`) trying to water their growing radishes. The 5-Day Forecast shows an incoming **Dry** spell.
* **The Action:**
  1. Switch to the **Hydration Lens (2)** to see how fast the land is drying.
  2. Select **Dig Swale (V)** tool (costs 5 Energy) to dig a trench next to your crops.
  3. When it rains (or if manually watered), the player watches the blue hydration aura spread outward via Capillary Action, saving the adjacent plants from the drought without spending daily Energy.

## Lesson 3: The Pig Tractor (Days 6-8)
* **The Concept:** Animals do the work for you.
* **The Pressure:** The player wants to expand, but cultivating and scything overgrown Yorkshire moorland drains massive amounts of Energy.
* **The Action:**
  1. Check the Bank: The player has earned a little cash from initial foraging/radish sales.
  2. Build a **Pig House (£80)** near the overgrown edge.
  3. Hit SLEEP and watch the floating `"Oink! (Clearing)"` text. The pig automatically cultivates the land, destroys the weeds, and injects Nitrogen and Macro-life into the soil.

## Lesson 4: Chop and Drop (Days 9-11)
* **The Concept:** Weeds and pioneers are just temporary biological batteries.
* **The Pressure:** The player tries to plant a demanding, high-value crop (like an Apple Tree), but the soil inspector warns `"Hungry!"` because Nitrogen is too low.
* **The Action:**
  1. Look at the Pioneer radishes or beans planted in Lesson 1.
  2. Instead of harvesting them, use the **Scythe (X)**.
  3. This triggers the "Chop and Drop" mechanic, killing the pioneer but permanently injecting its stored nutrients and breaking the `Clay` tag into `Aerated` loam. The soil is now healed.

## Lesson 5: Guilds & The Food Forest (Days 12-14)
* **The Concept:** Plants thrive in communities, not in isolation.
* **The Pressure:** The Apple Tree is growing, but very slowly.
* **The Action:**
  1. Open the **Plant Codex** to read about Guilds.
  2. Plant Broad Beans (Nitrogen Fixer) and Daikon (Clay Breaker) in the immediate tiles surrounding the Apple Tree.
  3. The game recognises the **"Orchard Trinity"** guild. The player sees the growth multiplier (`growth_mult: 2.0`) engage.
  4. Build an **Honesty Box (£25)** to automate the sales of the massive new yields. The food forest is officially self-sustaining.
