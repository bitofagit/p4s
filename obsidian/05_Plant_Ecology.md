# Plant & Soil Interactions (Ecology)
**Tags:** #mechanics #plants #ecology

Plants in Permaculture 4 Squares are not static objects; they are active participants in the soil ecosystem. They act as bridges between the [[01_Physical_Layer]], [[02_Chemical_Layer]], and [[03_Biological_Layer]].

## 1. What Plants Consume (The Inputs)
To survive and grow, a plant checks the local cell's metrics every turn:
* **[[Moisture]] & Fertility:** The primary fuels. Heavy feeders (e.g., Pumpkins) drain Fertility quickly.
* **[[pH Level]]:** The gatekeeper. If the soil pH is outside the plant's ideal range, it suffers a severe growth penalty, even if Fertility is 10/10.
* **Topsoil Depth:** Taproots require high depth; if planted in shallow soil, they stunt and die.

## 2. What Plants Produce (The Outputs)
As they grow, or when they die, plants alter the cell. **Daily exchange deltas scale with the plant’s current growth stage** (see [[26_Six_Stage_Growth_and_Dynamic_Exchange]]): a seedling barely drinks; vegetative growth peaks nitrogen draw; senescent plants stop feeding.
* **Canopy Shading:** Large plants reduce Moisture evaporation and block sunlight for the understory.
* **Nitrogen Fixing:** Pioneer species (e.g., Clover, Alder) actively *increase* Fertility over time.
* **Phytoremediation:** Specific accumulators (e.g., Sunflowers) lower Toxicity but become toxic themselves.

## 3. The Biological Drivers
The most important interaction is how a plant shapes the Soil Food Web.
* **Annuals (Vegetables):** Fast lifecycles. When they die and rot, they cause massive spikes in **Bacteria**.
* **Perennials (Trees/Shrubs):** Permanent roots. They secrete sugars that feed and slowly build up the **Fungal Load** over multiple seasons.
* **Taproots (Daikon/Comfrey):** Acting as bio-drills, they physically break up hardpan clay, permanently increasing **Structure** and **Macro-Life** access.
